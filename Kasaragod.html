Kasargod

Kasargod is located on the sea coast bordered by hilly Kodagu and Mangalore districts of Karnataka. Even in this era of great industrialization, here fishing is the chief source of livelihood. Along with this, people also work in handloom and coir sectors for earning their daily bread and butter. Furthermore owing to its natural and traditional richness, Kasargod is bestowed with numerous tourist attractions like the dazzling rivers, sandy beaches, splendid hills, ancient forts and sacred shrines.

Fortresses being the main attraction of the place are included in the itinerary. The first stopover of the tour is the Bekal Fort, standing on 35 acres headland that runs into the Arabian Sea. It is among the largest and the best preserved forts of Kerala. The fort is complemented with a zigzag entrance and the trenches that border it stand as an evidence of the defense strategy of yester years. Further cosseted by the soothing music of the sea waves, the fort is highly appreciated for its surroundings and ambience.

Top sights in Kasargod

Nileswaram
Nileswaram, once the cultural centre of Kasaragod District, is home to the Nileshwar Rajas, said to have come from Kozhikode nearly 600 years ago. Their palaces showcase the fine architecture and spectacular woodwork of the times. The Nileswaram Palace today functions as the folklore centre of the Department of Archaeology.
Kundamkuzhy Fort
Located in the Bandadka village in Kasaragod, the Kundamkuzhy Fort is a land fort that dates back to the time of the Bednur Nayaks. This square fort is made of laterite blocks, and rests in the valley of laterite hillocks. The fort was constructed to ease and facilitate trade from the coastal towns to the Mysore plateau.
Chandragiri Fort & River
Chandragiri Fort in Kasaragod lies at the confluence of the Payaswini River and the Arabian Sea. This 17th century fort was built by Sivappa Naik of Bedanore. Situated atop hill ruins. People come here to watch the sunset from the ancient Kizhur Siva Temple at Chandragiri. While the nearby Bekal Fort is a tourist hotbed.
Bekal Fort
Around 16 km to the south of northernmost district in Kerala, Kasaragod, lies the massive Bekal Fort. It is among the biggest forts in Kerala and has been impeccably maintained throughout the years. It rises to 130 feet above sea level and is situated on a headland spanning 35km. Much effort has been put to develop a beautiful beach here called the Bekal Fort Beach.
